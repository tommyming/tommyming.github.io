## "I have written a 2026 wish list, let's get a strong start in 2026!"

Meanwhile, it is March 2026 already, when I write this article.

I think most of the us would do the same thing: when we look back at the end of year. Most of the stuff are not checked, probably you have finished 1 or 2 targets, thats amazing, but you didn't do the rest.

That's normal for most of us, including me. 

I have been lazy on crafting articles in 2025, mostly because of AI. My daily workflow has changed a lot: You mostly wont search that much on Google right now. 

Instead, you are using multiple search tools. Apart from `Google Search`, you would probably have another tab that is a `chatbot window`, you will ask more detail questions, keeping conversations, asking follow-up questions. These are the thing you wont do before.

When you can search all the knowledge, distill it with chatbot, and "munch" them up, you will have less eager on writing/reading blogs. This is me until January 2026.

One night, when I try to recall the knowledge I munched from AI. My brain shows a very blurry image with bunch of question marks. Wait, aren't I digested the knowledge already? Why my knowledge still feels shallow? 

Therefore I decided to start writing again, no matter how short or long is. When you write, you are truly building up the knowledge you have just seen. Jotting notes is a way, writing articles is probably another way to record your own journey.
