### It all start with a very "niche" website...

![the advent of code website index page](https://images.mingtommy.dev/aoc-1.png)

Every year late November/December, you would like to sit-down in front of the computer, and checking the [wesbite](https://adventofcode.com) shown above.

__Advent Of Code__ is the Christmas Calendar for Programmers. You will start with a problem, which part 1 and 2 every day, in total of 12 questions.

That means, you can finish the thing on Dec 12! (Even if you do 1 part everyday, you can finish them on Christmas Eve, Dec 24.)

AoC is relatively different from the problem-solving question you will see on Leetcode. Take a look at __Day 1 2025__ , which is the picture below:
![problem day 1 of 2025](https://images.mingtommy.dev/aoc-2.png)

---

### When you started with this "Calendar"?

I found AoC on the internet in 2022(during covid). The questions are too hard for me at that year. (Yeah when there is stil no AI) But I still pretty hard every year, try to solve every Part 1 and understand what is happening within every question. Since then, complete the Christmas tree is my goal every year.

Until now, solving Part 1 of that day has become slightly easier for me. But still, some Part 2s are quite difficult.

I do use a bit of AI in 2025, for explaining to me what is happening in the question. But I still love to solve the question by myself. 90% of the questions are solve by myself, with lots of struggle and days of hard work, after work. 

### Why you love "Advent of Code"?

I love solving problems, especially challenging ones. The problem inside AoC do make me struggle and keeping searching for solution, which makes me love the feeling. 

And the most important part is, when you solved the problem, you will feel refreshed from this.

Most importantly, it is great to build a Christmas tree before Christmas, and it is fun!

### Last Words...
When dealing with challenging(or probably too challenging) problems, the problem itself, will reflect your problem-solving skills.

The approach of you solving the problem is precious, doing AoC can reveal where you are missing, and make you to become better.

### Side Note
Something I am worrying about is the use of AI. AI can actually solve the problems easily inside AoC, no matter part 1 or 2. And since more people using AI, submission spikes which causes the server load of AoC website increase.

I wonder how would AoC evolved in the future, or maybe probably this year.
